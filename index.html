My Process

Research

I believe that good design comes from good research and a lot of empathy. Understanding where a user is coming from is the cornerstone and building block of every design desicion that follows.

Wireframes

Wireframes guide the design of the user's flow. Low fidelity mockups, whether as sketches or wireframes, allow for more iterations at faster pass. The more I iterate the better the final outcome.

Design

Using Photoshop, Sketch, Flinto and other design tools I bring the wireframes to life with colors, fonts, sizing and layout tweaks. Theseout fidelity mockups are the core of the design and I enjoy getting them in front of user ASAP.

Code

As a designer I have the benefit of having a good handle on the development process. My understanding of HTML and CSS streamlines the communication between myself and the dev team and pushes me to design with clear purpose.

My Background

International/Creative/Social Science/Music






My name is Trevor Byron Borden. I am an Austin based UX/UI designer. I was born in Nairobi, Kenya and spent my growing up years in a remote Maasai village in the southern highlands called the Loita Hills. I lived in Lisbon, Portugal for six years before moving to California to study Art and Social Science at Westmont College.

For the past seven years I have spent my time doing creative work. I've toured the USA multiple times playing music as a singer-songwriter, worked as a high end Finish Carpenter in the mansions of Montecito, CA and I've planned, designed, recruited and guided summer Safaris in Tanzania. The work I've found most fufilling is that which creates a specific and intentional experience for others. Whether its creating a contimplative atmosphere for folks in a basement nightclub, putting in the finishing touches of a cozy breakfast knook, or anticipating the needs of first time visitors to Africa, I've exercised my empathy for others and have successfully learned how to create an intended experience for someone else.

I designed my first website in 2005 for a punk-rock band I was a part of and been using Photoshop ever since, designing logos for artists and non-profits, album art, t-shirts, stickers and posters. At the end of 2016 I decided to dive deeper in UI design and enrolled in a emmersive 3-month bootcamp at the Iron Yard in Austin. I've fallen in love with the design process and have loved learning new tools for creating intuitive and responsive websites and apps. My empathy and ability to anticipate other's experiences has equipt me to thrive as a UX/UI designer. I am thrilled to be here.
